State of the Map Africa 2019: Programme Design & Community Insights
Programme Insights Report

A data-driven exploration of participation, equity, and programme design at Africa’s State of the Map conference.

📌 Overview

This project presents a structured analysis of the Call for Proposals (CFP) and programme selection process for the 2019 State of the Map Africa conference.

The goal is to move beyond descriptive statistics and provide programme-level insights that inform:

Conference design decisions
Community engagement strategies
Equity and inclusion planning

🎯 Key Questions
Who is participating, and from where?
What types of sessions are being proposed?
How are programme selection decisions made?
What signals exist around equity and access?

🔍 Key Insights
Submission Concentration:
Submissions were heavily concentrated within a short time window, creating operational pressure during peak review periods.
Geographic Imbalance:
Participation was dominated by a small number of countries, indicating uneven engagement across the continent.
Format Bias:
Most submissions favored traditional presentation formats over interactive or participatory sessions.
Curated Selection:
Selection outcomes reflect deliberate programme design choices rather than purely neutral filtering.
Equity Signals:
High demand for financial support highlights structural barriers to participation.
